Data Journalism and D3

2014 U.S. State Data Analysis

The chart allows to implement the visualization of multiple variables

  • Independent variables: % Poverty or Age (Median) or household income (median)
  • Dependent variables: % obese population or % of smokers or % of the population without healthcare insurance

The data is presented grouped by states. Please select the axes categories you want to compare. You can hover over the state markers and the chart will show the values that they represent.

The comparison of the three dependent variables vs. median household income shows some interesting trends

  • Both % of obesity and % of smokers seems to be higher in states with lower median household income in comparison to states with higher income. The linear correlation coefficient are in the rage of 0.65-0.66, the highest values of the 3-comparison.
  • The % of uninsured population does not show an evident trend. States with lower household income shows relatively low % of insured population (likely due the Medicaid program). On the other, Massachussetts has the lower % of insured population. This might be related to the exitence of state-level program similar to the Affordable Care Act (ACA or theso-called Obama care) scope since 2006. Texas shows the higher % of uninsured population. After the implementation of the ACA, the state goverment did not expand the Medicad program target to the lower income population (please click here to see source). Other state slike Georgia and Mississippi also have not enacted the Medicaid expansion
  • The data we are showing is from 2014. Some states information have changed. For example, Loisiana reported about 8% of insured population in 2019 mainly due to reduced poverty percentage and extension of the Medicaid program by the state government (please click here to see source).

The trends of the comparison of the 3 dependent variables vs. the % of poverty will reflect the trends that we just mentioned for household income. States with lower % of poverty (therefore, higher median income) will show higher % of obesity and smokers

The comparison of the 3 dependent variables vs. age median does not show major evident patterns. The % of uninsured population seems to be mainly between 34 to 40 years. When selecting the % of smokers, Utah has the younger population and shows the lowest percentage of smokers, likely related to the high % of people professing the Mormon religion (please click here to see source)
